# Kape Kunana Brand Guide

## Brand Name

Kape Kunana

Meaning:

"Kunana" is an Ilocano word meaning "sabi nila."

The brand represents stories, conversations, experiences, and connections shared over coffee.

---

# Brand Tagline

A Cup That Completes Your Day.

Alternative:

Tara Kape — Sabi Nila.

---

# Brand Mission

To create a warm and welcoming space where people can enjoy quality coffee, meaningful conversations, creativity, and connection.

---

# Brand Vision

To become a recognizable specialty coffee brand known for quality drinks, memorable experiences, and authentic community connections.

---

# Brand Personality

Kape Kunana is:

* Warm
* Friendly
* Modern
* Relaxed
* Authentic
* Creative
* Welcoming

Never feel corporate.

Never feel cold.

Never feel overly luxurious.

Premium but approachable.

---

# Brand Voice

Tone:

* Conversational
* Friendly
* Natural
* Human
* Modern

Use simple language.

Avoid corporate buzzwords.

Avoid overly formal copy.

---

# Customer Experience

Visitors should feel:

* Relaxed
* Comfortable
* Welcome
* Inspired
* Connected

The website should feel like an invitation to stay.

Not just an invitation to buy coffee.

---

# Target Audience

Primary:

Age:
18–35

Interests:

* Coffee
* Study sessions
* Remote work
* Creative work
* Hangouts
* Photography
* Café culture

Secondary:

Age:
35–60

Interests:

* Quality coffee
* Relaxation
* Family time
* Community

---

# Core Brand Values

* Quality
* Honesty
* Community
* Creativity
* Consistency
* Hospitality

---

# Website Feel

The website should feel like:

* A premium local coffee shop
* Modern specialty café
* Cozy but professional
* Instagram-worthy
* Memorable

Users should immediately think:

"I want to visit this place."

---

# Photography Direction

Prioritize:

* Espresso shots
* Latte art
* Brewing process
* Barista moments
* Customer experiences
* Warm lighting
* Natural coffee colors

Avoid:

* Generic stock photos
* Overly staged imagery
* Artificial-looking visuals

---

# Copywriting Rules

Headlines should be:

* Short
* Emotional
* Memorable

Examples:

"Every Great Story Starts With Coffee."

"More Than Coffee. More Than Moments."

"Where Conversations Brew."

"Coffee Worth Staying For."

Subheadlines should support the headline without becoming too long.

---

# CTA Style

Preferred:

* Order Now
* View Menu
* Visit Kape Kunana
* Explore Our Best Sellers
* Start Your Coffee Journey

Avoid generic CTAs like:

* Submit
* Learn More
* Click Here

---

# Content Principles

Every page should communicate:

1. Quality Coffee
2. Community
3. Experience
4. Comfort
5. Authenticity

Never focus only on products.

Always sell the experience.

---

# Kape Kunana Golden Rule

People don't visit Kape Kunana just for coffee.

They visit for the feeling that comes with it.
